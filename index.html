Abstract

We propose HySAC, a hyperbolic safety-aware CLIP model that addresses the retrieval of unsafe content in vision-language systems. Existing unlearning-based techniques remove unsafe knowledge, but lose the ability to distinguish between safe and unsafe content. HySAC introduces a new paradigm by leveraging the hyperbolic space to encode an entailment hierarchy between safe and unsafe content. This enables structured representation, dynamic traversal of queries, and safe content redirection.

Experiments show that HySAC outperforms prior work in retrieval, classification, and redirection tasks, establishing a more flexible and interpretable framework for safety in multimodal AI.

Model Architecture

HySAC learns to encode text and images in a shared hyperbolic space using entailment cones and contrastive losses. Unsafe content is pushed further from the origin, forming a safety-aware hierarchy. A traversal mechanism allows redirecting unsafe queries to safe regions without discarding original semantics.

We validate the model using a synthetic and real-world benchmark across retrieval and generation tasks, demonstrating HySACâ€™s ability to preserve performance while enhancing safety and interpretability.

Tasks and Results

HySAC is evaluated on text-to-image and image-to-text retrieval, NSFW classification, and redirection of unsafe queries using safety traversal. It outperforms CLIP, Safe-CLIP, MERU, and HyCoCLIP in both recall and classification accuracy.

Notably, HySAC retrieves safe content when prompted with unsafe queries, and can retain unsafe results when needed. It generalizes to unseen NSFW categories and excels on real-world datasets.
